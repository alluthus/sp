Background

Dedicated to those who have lost their life to cancer, Joseph Madril's heartfelt instrumental ballad 'Somewhere Far Beyond' was created shortly after his grandmother, Karen Madril, lost her life to stage 4 pancreatic cancer. "When my grandmother was first diagnosed with cancer, it was a really complicated time for our family. A few years prior, my grandfather found out he had cancer, but it was detected early on and he was able to get the treatment he needed to get rid of the cancer. When I first found out, I thought it would be similar and she would be back to her normal self in no time."

Through research, Madril learned how difficult pancreatic cancer is to diagnose. "My grandmother originally had mild abdominal pain for almost a year prior to her diagnosis. She had a lot of allergies to many different things, so she assumed she was eating something she was allergic to. When she changed her diet, the pain went away."

Within the few months prior to her diagnosis, Madril's grandmother experienced extreme pain in her abdomen. "She eventually went to the emergency room because of the pain she had on her stomach. After lots of extensive testing, she was told there was cancer in her pancreas."

According to the National Cancer Institute (NCI), pancreatic cancer is complicated to diagnose early due to lack of noticeable signs or symptoms in its early stages. Signs and symptoms also are similar to other illnesses. "When she found out of the cancer, it was already at stage 4 and there was not much options for her to improve her condition. Despite heavy treatments with chemotherapy and radiation therapy, her expected survival rate was around one year."

Within a year and two months after her diagnosis, Madril's grandmother passed away in her home surrounded by family and friends. "She fought so hard to stay with us, for our grandfather, her children and grandchildren." After her passing, various members of Madril's family were inspired to help with early cancer detection research. "One of my aunts has contributed to the Healthy Oregon Project by voluntarily donating biological samples. Despite our advances in technology and medicine, there is still very little known about causes of cancer. A lot of research goes into finding a cure for cancer, but research is also needed for early detection so it can be treated easily during its initial stages. Early detection of cancer can save lives. When it is found early, the chance of survival and quality of life are much higher. Especially if cancer is common in your family, a routine screening can save your life." Madril explains that making healthy choices in lifestyle can reduce the risk of cancer. "Avoid smoking, have a healthy balanced diet, be active, maintain healthy weight, and wear sunscreen."

With the release of 'Somewhere Far Beyond', Madril hopes to spread awareness of the risks of cancer and further research for early screening on cancers that are difficult to detect early on. "Not only is pancreatic cancer hard to detect early, but also lung cancer, ovarian cancer, colorectal cancer, breast cancer, and so many others. My grandmother always helped those in need, and I want to do the same for those with similar battles and those at risk of developing cancer with little to no signs."
